Structured Intake Assistant

Internal working name for Erik's idea: a tool that could help administer or streamline a highly structured psychiatric intake/interview workflow inspired by SCID-style assessment, without pretending that Aspen Frost can casually ship clinical diagnostic software.

Status

  • Stage: Intake / Safety-Gated
  • Opened: 2026-06-24
  • Primary external stakeholder: Erik Frost
  • Initial source: Erik texted Andy about the SCID, a structured clinical interview for DSM-5 that is mostly used in research and can take at least an hour to administer.
  • Current gate: Gather remaining Andy/Erik context and confirm PDF provenance/licensing before any design or engineering commitment.
  • Approval: Draft / Aspen-created project record from Andy's Telegram request on 2026-06-24.

Working Project Shape

Erik is asking whether Aspen Frost could build something that administers, assists with, or makes practical use of a SCID-style structured psychiatric interview. His stated pain point is that a clinical assistant AI at work keeps suggesting the interview, but it is not practical because it takes at least an hour to administer.

The opportunity may be real, but this is health/clinical territory. The first job is not to build. The first job is to understand the actual workflow, licensing/IP constraints around the source instrument, clinical boundaries, user roles, data handling, and the smallest safe thing that would create value.

Safety Posture

Initial posture is Level 5: Critical stakes if the tool administers a clinical diagnostic interview, stores PHI, influences diagnosis, or is used in a care workflow.

It may be reducible to Level 4: High stakes only if scoped as an internal research/prototype or clinician-supervised workflow helper that avoids autonomous diagnosis, avoids real patient data, and has explicit privacy and review controls.

Do not treat this as a quick prototype for production clinical use.
