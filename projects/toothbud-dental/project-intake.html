ToothBud Dental - Project Intake

> Status: Draft > Owner: Andy + Aspen > Date opened: 2026-06-24 > Current gate: Intake / Safety-Gated > Source: Andy Telegram request on 2026-06-24.

1. Overview

Explore whether Aspen Frost should help Ann and Luc Dimick solve pediatric dental practice-management pain around OpenDental.

2. Background

Ann is a pediatric dentist. Luc is her husband and IT support. They use OpenDental and are unhappy with it, mostly because it is not well suited for pediatric dentistry. Andy plans to set up an interview with Luc.

OpenDental reference points:

  • Website: https://www.opendental.com/
  • GitHub organization: https://github.com/OpenDental

3. Motivation

The working motivation is to reduce operational friction for a pediatric dental practice. This could mean better workflows, configuration guidance, integration, automation, reporting, or product replacement. The right shape is unknown until Luc explains the real pain.

4. Goals

  • Understand the pediatric-specific workflow gaps in OpenDental.
  • Map the current system, integrations, hosting, backups, and support burden.
  • Identify whether OpenDental can be configured, extended, integrated with, forked, or replaced.
  • Understand the competitive landscape for pediatric dental practice-management software.
  • Decide whether Aspen Frost should proceed, pause, recommend buy/configure, or run a narrow spike.

5. Non-Goals

  • Replacing OpenDental by default.
  • Touching production patient data during intake.
  • Building a HIPAA-adjacent system before safety, compliance, migration, and support scope are explicit.
  • Promising clinical, billing, or operational reliability before architecture and SRE review.

6. Known Current State

  • Ann and Luc currently use OpenDental.
  • Ann practices pediatric dentistry.
  • They are unhappy with OpenDental's fit for pediatric workflows.
  • Luc is the planned first discovery interview.
  • OpenDental has a public website and GitHub organization.

7. Unknowns

  • Which exact pediatric workflows are painful.
  • Their practice size, hosting model, version, integrations, and IT constraints.
  • Whether they want replacement, augmentation, reporting, automation, or consulting.
  • What data migration/export options exist.
  • What compliance, backup, support, and vendor constraints apply.

8. Stakeholders & Users

  • Andy: Aspen Frost Visionary/CPO and intake gate.
  • Aspen: Integrator and project record owner.
  • Ann Dimick: Dentist and primary domain stakeholder.
  • Luc Dimick: IT support and first planned interview contact.
  • Practice staff: Possible users across scheduling, front desk, billing, assistants, hygienists, and clinical operations.
  • Patients/families: Indirectly affected if workflows touch communications, scheduling, forms, or records.

9. Stakes Ramp Declaration

Proposed Level

Level 5: Critical stakes for anything that touches live dental practice operations, PHI, patient records, billing/insurance, appointment scheduling, clinical documentation, imaging, patient communications, or data migration.

Early interview prep and public research are lower-risk if no real patient data is handled.

Reasoning

Dental practice-management systems are operationally critical and contain sensitive health and financial data. A bad migration, integration, or automation could disrupt care, revenue cycle, compliance, or patient trust.

10. Recommendation

Proceed with intake and research only. Use Arya and Petra's first-pass analyses to sharpen Luc interview questions and decide whether the right next move is configuration, integration, product selection, or a deliberately bounded prototype.
