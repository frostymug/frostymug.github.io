ToothBud Dental - Luc Interview Prep

> Status: Draft > Purpose: first discovery interview guide for Luc Dimick.

Interview Goal

Understand what is actually broken or painful about OpenDental in Ann's pediatric dentistry practice, before assuming the answer is new software.

Opening Context

  • This is discovery, not a commitment to build.
  • We should avoid patient-identifiable examples. Use anonymized/general workflow examples only.
  • The goal is to understand workflows, constraints, and decision criteria.

Questions

Practice and Setup

  • How many providers, operatories, locations, and staff are involved?
  • How is OpenDental hosted today: local server, cloud-hosted, vendor-hosted, or something else?
  • Which version/modules do you use?
  • What other systems are connected: imaging, payments, claims, texting, forms, patient portal, accounting, analytics?

Pain Points

  • What are the top 5 daily annoyances with OpenDental?
  • Which pain is specifically pediatric rather than general dental practice management?
  • Where do staff use workarounds, spreadsheets, paper, duplicate entry, or side systems?
  • What creates errors, delays, or patient/family frustration?
  • What does Ann complain about most after a normal clinic day?

Pediatric Dentistry Specifics

  • How do guardian/parent relationships work in the system?
  • Are siblings/family scheduling, consents, recalls, school forms, sedation, behavior notes, or age-specific workflows painful?
  • What pediatric reporting or clinical documentation does OpenDental not support well?
  • Are there child-specific insurance/billing quirks that cause trouble?

Data and Operations

  • What data would need to move if you switched systems?
  • Do you have reliable backups and test restores?
  • How hard is it to export data from OpenDental?
  • What downtime could the practice tolerate?
  • Who supports the system today when something breaks?

Buying vs Building

  • Have you evaluated alternatives? Which ones and why did they fail?
  • Would a better configuration, plugin, integration, or reporting layer solve 80% of the pain?
  • What would make you switch vendors?
  • What would make you stay on OpenDental?

Success Criteria

  • If this project worked, what would be different 90 days later?
  • What must never break?
  • What are the budget/time/support constraints?
  • Who besides Luc and Ann needs to be involved before any decision?

Red Flags To Listen For

  • Any request to copy production data into our workspace.
  • Any expectation that Aspen Frost can casually replace an EHR/practice-management system.
  • No backup/export path.
  • Billing/claims or clinical documentation dependencies that are not understood.
  • Staff adoption problems masquerading as software problems.
