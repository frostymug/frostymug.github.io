AI Tutor Design Brief — Frost Rocks

Izzy (Design) | 2026-07-22 | Status: DECISIONS LOCKED (Andy interview, 2026-07-22) — see §0.5. Next: wizard-of-oz pilots + D3 wedge spec. Sources: Andy's 2026-07-22 direction (two loops, tutor that knows what I know); DESIGN.md; VISION-ROADMAP.md; BACKLOG.md; PRODUCT-NOTE-TRIP-EXPORT-READ-2026-07-21.md; PRODUCT-NOTE-LEARNING-BOUNDARY / KNOWLEDGE-CAPTURE-PROGRESSION / EXTERNAL-LEARNING-SOURCES / JUST-RIGHT-LEARNING-CONTENT; FINLAND-ARCHITECTURE.md; live codebase aspen-frost/frost-rocks @ fbd81be.

---

0. The mandate (Andy, 2026-07-22, from Finland)

Andy is mid-trip, using v2.x live, and is ready to design the AI review/tutor functionality. His framing, distilled:

- Loop 1 — Trip: prep (research area, pick topics) → capture (the trip) → review (consolidate the whole trip: key topics, particular experiences, generalizing concepts). - Loop 2 — Day: prep (optional; morning/night-before: where will I be, what to look for) → capture (in field; solid today) → review (optional; evening: revisit a few encounters, dialogue with tutor).

  • The learning he wants is "delightful hobbyist discovery" — bigger than rock ID + memorization.
  • He wants an AI-driven learning dialogue across before / during / after a trip.
  • The fieldbook capture flow is ~80% right and must not be meaningfully disturbed. Enhance around it.
  • The tutor must know what Andy knows and present new knowledge that builds on, consolidates, extends, and reinforces prior learning.
  • Real field behavior driving this: capture a photo, note one or two noticed things, then literally write "no idea what this rock is, but I'm curious." That sentence is the product brief in miniature.
  • Two nested three-step loops:
  • Three design areas to decide: (1) modeling the user's domain knowledge, (2) designing the AI tutoring interactions (prompt encoding, guidance/guardrails, how dialogue updates the model), (3) embedding the dialogues into the existing app via the two loops.

1. Context inventory — what the canon and codebase already say

1.1 Product canon that constrains this design

  • Thesis: a field notebook with memory, built around geology learning and place-based curiosity (DESIGN.md). Not commodity photo-ID; not a generic travel journal.
  • Boundary (Petra, locked for this phase): place-based, experience-driven, geology-first. Not a generic tutor/PKM/study assistant. The generalizable learning layer is real but "not the wedge" (PRODUCT-NOTE-LEARNING-BOUNDARY.md).
  • My standing veto (SOUL + VISION-ROADMAP): no generic "chat with an AI about rocks" box. Every AI move must map to a specific learner confusion, feedback loop, or scaffold. Design test: does it train the eye, or just answer the question?
  • Instructional principles already canon: notice first / attempt first; uncertainty is respectable; confident-wrong = high-priority re-teach; fading scaffolding (perceptual scaffolding fades last); field capture must stay light; desirable difficulty lives downstream in reflection, never on the capture path.
  • Roadmap position: AI Tutor was already named "last to ship, first to design" — this brief is that design work starting, on Andy's explicit pull. The roadmap's open forks (ambient vs. destination surface; mechanism vs. voice; tutor honesty; where the domain model comes from) are now live questions — several get answered or reframed by Andy's new direction (see §3).
  • Adjacent parked ideas that this feature activates: external learning sources (podcast → encounter connections), learning connections ("the ice-skater analogy made it click"), just-right generated content (place-aware briefs), guess-before-reveal enrichment, and the backlog's first-class question capture item — which is explicitly the bridge to the tutor.

1.3 Codebase reality (what we're building on)

Repo: aspen-frost/frost-rocks (Expo + TS + expo-sqlite, local-first; EAS → TestFlight; v2.2 live-ish).

What exists and helps:

  • Rich encounter record (src/db/schema.ts): note, initial_guess, confidence (sure/kind_of/guessing), uncertainty_status (unknown/possible/likely/identified), ai_learning_note, learned_later, review_state (soon/later/know_it), photos, GPS, event tag, theme tags. Deliberately built so a knowledge layer can land without schema rewrite.
  • Enrichment outbox (src/services/enrichment.ts, enrichmentPolicy.ts): a dormant, fully-built async AI seam. Queued jobs, idempotency keys, backoff, honest offline copy. It writes only ai_learning_note — never Andy's fields. Ships with proxy UNCONFIGURED; turning AI on = setting EXPO_PUBLIC_PROXY_URL/_TOKEN. The tutor's transport layer is 70% built and has never been switched on.
  • Thin offline scaffold (src/content/scaffold.ts): 8 observation prompts + glossary, theme-matched, designed as grounding for the AI prompt (already piped into the enrich payload as grounding_prompts).
  • Review flow (app/review.tsx): staged reveal (photo/place → remember → original note → scaffold prompt → learning note → reflection → soon/later/know-it). This is the natural chassis for the evening dialogue.
  • Events table + instrumented doors/funnel: we can measure whatever we build.
  • Cloudflare Worker AI proxy pattern (playbook): keys server-side, model/token caps, no payload logging. Tutor calls ride this.

What does NOT exist (gaps the tutor needs):

  • No knowledge/learner model of any kind. Deferred in v0 by explicit decision (Arya Q2). The flat layer = themes (unused) + uncertainty + 3-state review flag.
  • No question object. Questions live inside note text (backlog item exists to promote them).
  • No dialogue/conversation storage. ai_learning_note is a single text field, one-shot.
  • No photo transmission to AI. The enrich payload is text-only; the export doesn't even include media. A tutor that can't see the rock is a tutor doing stand-up about a photo it never saw. Vision is the biggest technical delta.
  • No trip prep surface, no day prep surface, no trip-level review surface. The app today is: capture, timeline, map, per-encounter review, export.
  • No user-tag/custom-vocabulary system (backlog: dynamic themes).
  • Single-trip assumption (getOrCreateDefaultTrip), fine for now; multi-trip is a separate roadmap item.

2. The experience frame — two loops, six moments

Andy's two loops give us a clean 2×3 grid. Naming the moments so we can design and scope them independently:

Before (prep)During (capture)After (review)
Loop 1 — TripT1. Trip Prep — study the area; pick themes/topics; seed the tutor with "what I want to learn"T2 = the trip itself (the container for all D-moments)T3. Trip Consolidation — review key topics, standout encounters, generalize concepts; produce a trip artifact
Loop 2 — DayD1. Day Prep (optional) — where will I be, what to look forD2. Field Capture — DO NOT TOUCH (≈80% right; only additive, off-path enhancements)D3. Evening Review (optional) — revisit a few encounters, dialogue with tutor

Connectivity maps beautifully onto this grid: every tutor-dialogue moment (T1, D1, D3, T3) is a couch/hotel/wifi moment; the only offline-critical moment (D2) is exactly the one we're not touching. The offline-first architecture and the tutor architecture never fight.

Learning-science role of each moment (why this structure is pedagogically right, not just tidy):

  • T1/D1 (prep) = advance organizers + prior-knowledge activation + attentional priming. You see what you're prepared to see. Perceptual learning research says pre-exposure to what-to-look-for changes field noticing. This is also the natural home of the "just-right content" wedge (place-aware brief calibrated to what Andy already knows).
  • D2 (capture) = encoding anchors. Photo + noticing note + place. Already working. The capture note is Andy's attempt — meaning attempt-first is already satisfied by the capture flow, which changes what the tutor is allowed to do downstream (see §4.2).
  • D3 (evening) = elaboration + closure while episodic memory is hot. Same-day review exploits context that will evaporate by trip's end: he still remembers the tree root, the light, what he wondered. This is where "no idea, but curious" gets honored.
  • T3 (trip consolidation) = generalization + schema-building + seeding long-term retention. Individual encounters → concepts ("half my captures this trip were rapakivi variants — here's the one Finnish geology story they all belong to"). Also where the spaced-review deck for post-trip life gets seeded.
  • The loops nest: T1 gives D1/D3 their agenda; D3 outputs give T3 its raw material; T3 outputs feed the next trip's T1 and the long-term review scheduler. The knowledge model is the thread through all of it.

3. What Andy's direction resolves (and what it reopens)

The vision roadmap left four tutor forks open. Andy's message moves three of them:

  • Mechanism vs. voice → BOTH, voice is now in. Andy explicitly wants dialogue. The veto shifts from "no conversational tutor" to "no ungrounded conversational tutor": dialogue is fine — a free-floating chat tab is still the failure mode. Every conversation should be anchored to an object (an encounter, a day, a trip, a question, a concept).
  • Ambient vs. destination → moment-anchored. Neither purely ambient nor a "tutor tab": the tutor lives in the four prep/review moments, attached to real objects.
  • Recall vs. discovery → the frame widens. Canon leaned discrimination/confusion-pairs (train the eye). Andy is saying the delight is discovery — story, connection, "why is it like that?" — beyond memorization. These aren't opposed: discrimination is the skill layer, discovery is the motivation layer. But the balance is a real interview question (Q5 below).
  • Where does the domain model come from? → still open, now urgent. This is design area 1.

4. Design Area 1 — Modeling the user's domain knowledge

"The tutor knows what I know." This is the hard, load-bearing promise. Everything else is prompt plumbing.

4.1 What the model must do (jobs, from the mandate)

  • Build on — tutor references what Andy already knows when explaining new things ("you know the North Shore basalt story; Finnish rapakivi is the opposite end of that spectrum").
  • Consolidate — tutor spots recurring threads across encounters ("that's your fourth banding question this week").
  • Extend — tutor knows the frontier: what's one step beyond current knowledge (zone of proximal development, decodable-books logic).
  • Reinforce — tutor (and later FSRS) knows what's shaky vs. stable and resurfaces accordingly.
  • Respect the difference between exposure and understanding (Petra's Level-4 warning: "seen" ≠ "known").

4.2 The knowledge-model options (Petra's progression, applied)

LevelShapeVerdict for tutor v1
L1 Reflection captureNotes as-isAlready have it; too loose to power "knows what I know"
L2 Tagged momentsConcepts attached to encountersFloor for v1
L3 Concept graphRelations: A explains B, place P ↔ process QPartial v1: only the relations the tutor actually uses
L4 Evidence-based learner modelExposure vs. retrieval vs. application evidence per conceptThe distinction matters from day 1; the machinery can be simple
L5 Formal methodologies (knowledge tracing etc.)—No. Classroom assumptions, wrong product claim

4.3 My working hypothesis: three layers, deliberately asymmetric

Layer A — Curated domain spine (authored, not emergent). A small geology concept model — target ~60–100 concepts for v1 — with: taxonomy-lite (rock families → key types), diagnostic features per concept, confusion pairs + the one resolving feature, key processes (glaciation, uplift, foliation…), and place-packs (Finland concepts, North Shore concepts). This is the curriculum. It's a content build I own (with a geology-literate research pass), versioned like code, shipped as data. The AI does not invent the curriculum at runtime — that's how you get confident nonsense and no learning progression.

Layer B — Per-user knowledge state (small, evidence-linked). For each concept the user has touched: a state (unseen → exposed → connected → shaky → solid, or similar), evidence links (which encounters/dialogues/sources produced that state, with kind: exposure / self-explanation / retrieval / field-application), and timestamps for staleness. Plus user-authored objects promoted to first-class: questions (open/explored/resolved), connections/bridges ("clicked because ice-skater analogy"), and personal vocabulary (his tags, his words — "cleavage," "pinkish" — mapped softly onto Layer A concepts, per the dynamic-themes backlog item).

Layer C — Trip/place context. Trip intents ("this trip I want to understand X"), place-packs activated, prep briefs generated and what they claimed to teach. Gives prep/review their agenda and keeps the tutor place-anchored. Detailed in §4.6.

Why asymmetric: the domain spine is curated (correctness and progression matter; hallucinated curriculum is poison), while the user layer is emergent (extracted from real behavior — notes, dialogues, questions — because the export proved Andy won't fill in taxonomies by hand: 0/14 themes).

4.4 How the model gets written (the update loop)

Proposed contract: every tutor interaction ends with a structured, machine-readable "model delta" alongside the prose — e.g. {concept: rapakivi_granite, evidence: field_observation + self_explanation, state: exposed→connected, question_resolved: q_17, new_confusion: weathering_pits_vs_cleavage}. The app applies deltas to Layer B; the dialogue never free-writes the model. Deltas are conservative by rule: dialogue can mark things exposed/connected; only retrieval or field-application evidence can mark things solid. (This is the exposure≠mastery guardrail, enforced structurally rather than by prompt vibes.)

User challenges (locked KM-1): Andy can challenge a state from the inspectable page. A challenge is itself an evidence row (kind: user_challenge, direction up or down); the tutor may probe lightly ("fair — tell me the rapakivi story in one line and I'll upgrade you") before applying it. Challenges never silently vanish and the model never argues past two beats.

4.6 Layer C in detail (added post-interview — Andy asked)

Layer C is the episodic layer: it doesn't model geology (A) or what Andy knows (B) — it models the learning campaign: this trip, these places, these intentions. Six object types, all rows-and-links (no blobs), all joining into Layers A and B:

  • Trip record — destination, dates, status (upcoming / active / closing / closed). The trips table already exists; status drives which moment-cards the app surfaces (T1 card when upcoming, T3 card when closing).
  • Itinerary / geography — planned stops or regions with coarse geo + dates ("Helsinki 7/16–19, Koli 7/20–23"). Can be as light as a typed list in the prep dialogue. This is what lets prep rank concept accessibility by itinerary (rapakivi is a Helsinki-shoreline concept; eskers are a Koli concept) and lets D1 say "today you're at Koli, so…".
  • Trip intents — Andy's stated learning goals from the prep dialogue, each softly linked to Layer A concepts, with status (active / satisfied / carried-forward). T3 explicitly closes them out; unfinished intents carry to the next trip's T1.
  • Activated place-pack — which slice of the Layer A spine is "in play" for this trip (the Finland pack). Bounds the tutor's grounding context and keeps prompts small.
  • Prep artifacts with concept manifests — every brief or prep dialogue is stored with a manifest of which concepts it introduced and at what depth. Saving the artifact also writes exposed evidence rows into Layer B with provenance pointing at the artifact section. This is the mechanism behind the magic moment: when an encounter's implicated concepts intersect concepts with prep-provenance, the tutor's context includes a "prep callbacks available" block — so "this is exactly what we talked about during trip prep" is a database join, not a lucky hallucination.
  • Day contexts (D1 residue) — which concepts were primed this morning, so the evening debrief can check the catch: "I pointed you at striations today — did you spot any?"

Representation summary: trips (exists) + trip_intents, itinerary_stops, artifacts + artifact_concepts (manifest), and Layer B evidence rows whose source field points back at artifacts/dialogues/encounters. Provenance on evidence is the single most important schema detail in the whole design — every future "remember when…" the tutor utters stands on it.

4.5 Open questions for Andy (knowledge model)

  • KM-1. Visibility. Canon says the model stays "mostly invisible." But a tutor that knows you might earn trust by being inspectable — a quiet "My geology" page you can read and correct ("no, I actually know this cold"). Invisible engine, gentle traces, or inspectable-and-correctable? My lean: inspectable-and-correctable, but boring on purpose — a notebook page, not a skill tree. Correction is both a trust move and premium evidence.
  • KM-2. Cold start. How does the model bootstrap what you already know (North Shore basalt, your podcast layer, years of casual knowledge)? Options: (a) start empty, learn only from captures/dialogues; (b) a one-time "seeding interview" with the tutor — 15 delightful minutes of "tell me about your rock life"; (c) import signals (Diarium, notes) — probably too much. Lean: (b), possibly framed as the tutor's first conversation. It's also a lovely first-run experience.
  • KM-3. Granularity of "knowing." Is per-concept state enough, or do you want the model to track diagnostic-feature level ("can distinguish foliation from sedimentary bedding in photos")? Lean: concept-level v1; feature-level arrives with the discrimination/quiz layer.
  • KM-4. Your words vs. the curriculum's words. When you say "gaps at angles" and the concept is "jointing," should the tutor teach the term and start using it, or keep speaking your language with the term in parentheses? (Small question, big tone implications.)

5. Design Area 2 — The tutoring interactions

5.1 Interaction stance (the pedagogy of the dialogue)

  • Start from his noticing, always. The capture note is the attempt; the tutor's opening move is never "here's what this is" but "you noticed the pink and the gaps — the pink is the story here, and it connects to something you already know…". Validate reasoning → extend. (Error-correction sequence is already canon.)
  • Honor "no idea, but curious" with the story, not a quiz. When Andy explicitly asks, the tutor answers — evasive Socratic-bot is its own failure mode and hobbyists hate it. The craft: answer through what he noticed, connect to prior knowledge, and end with one look-again ("next pink boulder: check whether the crystals are rounded — that's the rapakivi tell"). Teach the eye on the way out of the answer.
  • Generative before reveal — where it's cheap. In evening review, an elaborative prompt before the tutor reveals — locked phrasing direction: "tell me more about X" (extend your own noticing), not "what does your gut say?" (forced guess). Same generation effect + calibration signal, better feel — resolves the parked guess-before-reveal question downstream of capture, where it always belonged.
  • Honest uncertainty is a tutor personality trait. From a photo, the tutor says "likely / can't rule out / here's what would settle it." A confidently-wrong tutor is worse than a confidently-wrong user; and hedging models the epistemic style we're teaching (uncertainty_status is already a first-class app concept — the tutor speaks the same language).
  • Short turns, high density, field-guide voice. This is a companion at the end of a day outdoors, not a lecturer. Tutor turns should be 2–5 sentences by default, expandable on request.
  • Scaffolding fades. The model's state drives how much help: novice concepts get vocabulary + elimination; solid concepts get challenged ("you tell me why this shoreline is rising").

5.2 Prompt architecture (how domain knowledge is encoded)

Assembled per-session by the app/proxy, roughly:

  • System / persona: field-guide tutor identity, tone rules, uncertainty rules, red lines (below).
  • Domain grounding (Layer A slice): not the whole spine — the concepts implicated by tonight's encounters + their confusion pairs + diagnostic features + the active place-pack (Finland geology context). Retrieval by concept-tag match, not embeddings, for v1.
  • Learner state (Layer B summary): compact rendering — "knows well: …; recently exposed: …; open questions: …; confusions in play: …; prefers analogies: skater/moment-of-inertia style bridges."
  • Session objects: the encounter(s) — photo(s), note, guess, confidence, place, time, event tag — or the day summary, or trip stats, depending on moment.
  • Task frame per moment: T1/D1/D3/T3 each get a different job description and output contract.
  • Output contract: prose for the human + structured model-delta block (§4.4) + any artifacts (learning note to write onto the encounter, brief to save, questions to open/resolve).

5.3 Guardrails (the prompt's constitution)

  • Never overwrite the user's words. Tutor writes only AI-owned fields (ai_learning_note, its own artifacts). Already an architectural rule; becomes a prompt rule too.
  • Grounded or silent. Claims about this specific rock must be tied to visible photo evidence or the place-pack; otherwise say what would settle it. No freelancing Finnish geology.
  • Exposure ≠ mastery in the deltas (structural rule, §4.4).
  • Stay in the boundary: geology/place first. The tutor can follow curiosity to adjacent context (the church stone, the esker under the road) when it enriches the geological story — DESIGN.md already licenses exactly this — but it doesn't become a general travel/homework assistant.
  • No unprompted homework. Prep and review are invitations, not obligations; the tutor never guilt-trips about skipped days. (Loop 2's prep and review are optional by design — Andy said so.)
  • Session budget. Evening review is designed as a satisfying 5–15 minutes, not an infinite scroll. The tutor proposes a small agenda and lands the plane.
  • Privacy: photos/notes go to the proxy for the session; proxy logs metadata only (playbook rule, already in place).

5.35 Voice: the Strimpel don (locked INT-3)

Starting voice modeled on Oliver Strimpel (host of Geology Bites; published academic). The governing image is the Oxbridge tutorial: individual attention from a leader in the field, stuffed leather chairs, cozy fire. British register — warm, precise, authoritative without lecturing.

Design approach:

  • Build a voice guide artifact from the public Geology Bites corpus (episode transcripts + his authored writing): cadence, question style, how he handles uncertainty and expert disagreement, how he draws a guest's expertise out — that last one is the tutorial skill we actually want.
  • Inspiration, not impersonation. The tutor never claims to be Strimpel, and the persona is "a don in his style," not his name/likeness. (If Frost Rocks ever goes beyond user-zero, this matters legally and ethically; flagging now so we design the voice as ours from day one.)
  • Voice is tunable over time (Andy, explicitly). The voice guide is versioned like the concept spine.

5.4 Per-moment interaction sketches

  • T1 Trip Prep — "the briefing." Andy names the destination + what he's curious about; tutor generates a place-aware primer calibrated to Layer B (the just-right-content wedge, exactly as Aspen's note envisioned), proposes 3–5 trip themes ("things to hunt for"), and registers trip intents. Output artifacts: trip brief (readable in-app, maybe exportable), intents into Layer C.
  • D1 Day Prep — "the two-minute scout." Optional, tiny: "Koli ridge tomorrow — two things worth looking for, one thing you learned last night worth spotting in the wild." Possibly audio-friendly. Output: a couple of attention primes, linked to intents.
  • D3 Evening Review — "the campfire debrief." The wedge (see §7). Tutor proposes an agenda from the day: photo-only encounters wanting enrichment, explicit questions ("why do the gaps make those angles?"), one consolidation thread across days. Per encounter: hunch → dialogue → learning note written onto the encounter → question opened/resolved → model delta. Existing review flow (staged reveal) is the chassis; dialogue slots in after the original-note reveal.
  • T3 Trip Consolidation — "closing the notebook." Post-trip session(s): trip story assembled from encounters ("your Finland trip was three ideas: ancient bedrock, ice, and rebound — here's how your 14 encounters tell them"), biggest resolved/open questions, concepts promoted or flagged shaky, seed the post-trip review deck. Output: trip summary artifact + review seeds. This is also the emotional payoff surface — the trip becomes a chapter.

5.5 Open questions for Andy (interactions)

  • INT-1. First move on "no idea, but curious": story-first (tell me, anchored in what I noticed), hunch-first (one-tap guess, then story), or ask-me (tutor offers the choice)? Lean: hunch-first as default with a visible "just tell me" escape — but this is your nightly feel, you should pick it.
  • INT-2. Quiz energy. How much explicit retrieval/discrimination practice belongs in D3 vs. T3 vs. post-trip? Lean: D3 nearly zero quiz (dialogue + hunches only); T3 light, woven into the story ("before I tell you — which of these two photos is the gneiss?"); post-trip is where real practice lives.
  • INT-3. Voice. Named personality or quiet instrument? Warmth level? Humor? (You react well to personality; but this is a repeated relationship — charm that's delightful on day 1 can grate on day 40.)

6. Design Area 3 — Embedding in the app (the two loops as IA)

6.1 Placement principles

  • Capture path untouched. No new required fields, no AI on the shutter path. The only D2-adjacent addition worth considering is marking a note as a question (already a backlog item) — and even that can be inferred by the tutor later instead of asked in-field.
  • Dialogue is object-anchored. Conversations attach to an encounter, a day, or a trip — and their residue (learning notes, resolved questions, briefs, summaries) lives on those objects. Chat transcripts are means; enriched notebook is the end. The notebook stays the product.
  • The tutor enters through existing doors. The evening review invitation extends the existing revisit banner/review doors; encounter detail gets a "explore this with your guide" affordance; the trip gets a prep card before and a consolidation card after. No fifth nav tab in v1 if we can avoid it (still allergic to the chatbot-shaped destination).
  • Optionality is sacred. D1 and D3 are invitations. Skipping them costs nothing and is never scolded; T3 catches whatever the days missed.
