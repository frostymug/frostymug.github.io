Frost Rocks — v2.2 note: Compress the home map card

Izzy (Design) | 2026-07-10 | For: Jesse (impl). Small follow-up to FINLAND-V2.1-HOME-DELTA.md. Status: READY — one visual change, no logic, no data, no deps.

What's good (leave alone)

v2.1 landed the home layout the way we wanted: the stat-tile header is gone, the ambient revisit banner is in, the bottom nav is right (Notebook · Map · [Capture] · Review), and the field-guide skin looks great. Andy likes this screen. This note is one refinement only.

The one issue

The offline map card eats the whole fold. On Andy's screenshot it's ~55–60% of the viewport, so the only thing above the fold is header → banner → map. The timeline spine — the whole structural point of v2.1 — is entirely below the fold. You have to scroll past a giant map to reach the actual field notebook.

That's a sizing miss, and it traces back to the prototype vs. build gap: the prototype's map was always a compact card, not the full offline map inline.

The target (from the prototype — this was always the intent)

In the composite prototype (published-html/finland-v2-composite/index.html) the home map is a fixed-height compact card, explicitly commented:

<!-- Compact map card: emotional architecture, present but shrunk (Q6) -->
<div class="mapcard">
  <svg viewBox="0 0 440 150" ...>   <- 440x150: a short, wide thumbnail

Key properties of the prototype card:

route thumbnail, not a browsable map.

grow to fill available height.

Open map › link on the right.

raster/browsable map is the Map tab / the Open action. We don't need the full raster on the home fold — that's what the dedicated Map surface is for.

  • Short + wide. ~150px tall on a ~440px-wide phone -> roughly a 2.9 : 1 aspect ratio. It's a
  • .mapcard svg { height: auto } — it scales to width at that fixed short aspect; it does not
  • A caption bar under it (.mapcap): Koli · your route so far · tap a pin to revisit + a
  • Full map lives behind "Open". The compact card is the glanceable anchor; the real offline

The change for Jesse

In app/index.tsx, the home map card:

(width : height), i.e. roughly 130-160px tall on a standard phone width. Don't let it size to the raster's natural/full height. (RN: wrap the map in a View with a fixed height or aspectRatio: 2.9 and overflow: 'hidden', and crop/scale the raster to fit — center on the route rather than showing the whole tile sheet.)

unchanged; it keeps the full offline raster. We're only shrinking the home preview, not the map feature.

  • Cap its height. Give the map view a fixed short aspect — target ~2.8-3 : 1
  • Keep the caption bar below it (Koli · your route so far · tap a pin to revisit + Open map ›).
  • Keep the pins tappable on the compact card (Door A — door:'map' — unchanged).
  • Full map stays reachable via the Map nav tab and the Open link — that surface is

Net effect: header -> revisit banner -> compact map (~150px) -> theme chips -> timeline visible above the fold. The map becomes an orienting glance, and the notebook is the hero again.
