E1 — 27cc50b9 · Keskuspuisto Laakson Metsäalue · Mon 2026-07-20 06:41 local

> Those cracks are joints, not cleavage — they cut across the banding at their own angle because jointing comes from later stress relief (unloading as erosion/ice stripped kilometers of cover), indifferent to the rock's internal fabric. Cleavage follows fabric; joints ignore it. The foliation bands sitting at slightly different angles are the second story: wavy, discordant banding is the signature of migmatite — gneiss that partially melted ~1.9 Ga and swirled before refreezing. Look-again: at the next outcrop, find the cracks and check — do they respect the stripes or ignore them?

E2 — bdb6474a · Nuuksio hike · Mon 2026-07-20 10:51 local

> The blocky pink shapes are potassium feldspar, not quartz — and the tell is cleavage at crystal scale: feldspar breaks along flat internal planes, so its faces flash like tiny mirrors when you tilt the rock in sunlight; quartz has no cleavage and stays glassy/greasy like smoked glass. Also: in granite these aren't "inclusions" — the whole rock is interlocking crystals that grew together as the melt froze. Field test (30 seconds): tilt the next coarse pink rock in the light — what flashes is feldspar, what stays greasy is quartz. > Learner's own mnemonic (keep): "flashy feldspar cleaves differently than greasy quartz."

E3 — 0686340a · Fanninpenger 1 (wall block, walk to Maren's) · Sun 2026-07-19 20:33 local

3. Design findings (feed into D3 wedge spec)

  • F1 — Photo→encounter matching is the pilot's #1 friction, and it produced a real error. Webchat strips EXIF; wizard matched on setting/notes and mis-shelved E2 as note-less until Andy corrected. In-app D3 never has this problem (record travels with the dialogue). WoZ protocol fix: Andy sends photo + time/place in the same message. Product implication: none — this friction is an artifact of the wizard setup, don't over-engineer for it.
  • F2 — Hunch-first lands when it's honest data-gathering, not quiz theater. The "eggs or blocks?" question was genuinely needed (photo insufficient); Andy answered willingly. The "just tell me" escape went unused all session. Supports INT-1 as designed.
  • F3 — Spaced concept callbacks are the magic. Cleavage taught at outcrop scale (E1) → reused at crystal scale (E2) minutes later; foliation (E1) → carried the E3 verdict. Andy's engagement visibly spiked at these moments ("super helpful!"). Direct evidence for the concept-spine reuse hypothesis and the prep-provenance callback bet (§0.5 T1).
  • F4 — Learner self-generated a mnemonic and named it "solid calibration." The generative step happens naturally inside dialogue; no forced quiz needed (supports INT-2). Capture user-coined mnemonics as first-class Layer B evidence.
  • F5 — "Productively wrong" framing explicitly praised. Error-correction tone validated; add to voice guide vocabulary.
  • F6 — Learner challenge → tutor recalibration worked live (KM-1 in miniature). Andy pushed on ovoid size; tutor owned the overshoot and gave a tighter calibration ("cherry to wine-cork"), and the correction strengthened trust rather than denting it. Honest-uncertainty personality confirmed as a feature.
  • F7 — Showing work on the learner's own photo builds trust. Zoomed crops used as verdict evidence (E3). Candidate D3 interaction: tutor highlights/zooms the diagnostic region of the user's photo.
  • F8 — Voice held. Fireside don, one look-again per encounter, honest hedging, occasional wit. No grating detected in a 30-min session; the 40-day-charm question (§5.5 INT-3 note) remains open.
  • F9 — Session shape worked: per-encounter rhythm (context reinstatement → elaborative prompt → reveal → look-again) + a session-spanning thread (pink/K-feldspar; texture-as-plot). "Land the plane" discipline never tested — session ended externally.
