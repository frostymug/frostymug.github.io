Product Note — Just-Right Learning Content

Aspen | 2026-07-12 | Source: Andy Finland trip thought, connecting Frost Rocks to Michelle Hosp / decodable-books conversations.

Prompt

Andy raised a long-term Frost Rocks vision idea while preparing for the Finland trip and studying Finnish geology.

At work, Andy and Michelle Hosp have talked about AI-generated "decodable books" for kids: reading content generated around the phonemic patterns a learner has already mastered and the patterns they need to practice next. While studying geology, Andy noticed the same product pattern could apply far beyond early reading.

The generalized idea: generate custom learning content — book, essay, podcast, field guide, quiz, conversation, or other format — that leverages the user's existing knowledge while stretching the exact ideas, contrasts, and patterns they need next.

Andy described this as "just right" learning content. Not necessarily a totally novel idea, but it felt novel and productively cool in the Frost Rocks context.

Product Insight

Frost Rocks may eventually be able to generate learning material that is calibrated to the user's personal knowledge state, not just the domain curriculum.

For geology, this could mean:

  • A short Finland geology primer that assumes Andy already understands the North Shore basalt story.
  • A comparison essay that uses familiar Lake Superior examples to explain Finnish bedrock and glacial features.
  • A field-day audio brief that previews the concepts likely to matter at tomorrow's location.
  • A review story or podcast episode that deliberately works the user's current confusion pairs, such as granite vs. gneiss or basalt vs. gabbro.
  • A micro-field guide that reuses the user's own photos and observations as examples.

The important move is not "AI generates content." The important move is AI generates content from the intersection of domain model, learner model, place/context, and upcoming experience.

Why It Belongs In Frost Rocks

This extends the Vision Roadmap's AI Tutor idea, but it is not exactly the same thing.

The tutor roadmap focuses on diagnosis, confusion pairs, review, and field scaffolding. This note adds a content-generation layer: once the app knows what the user knows, where they are going, what they have captured, and what they are currently struggling with, it can create a learning artifact at the right level.

That could make Frost Rocks feel less like a study app and more like a personalized naturalist companion: it prepares you before the field, sharpens your eye during the trip, and turns your own encounters into better learning after the fact.

Strategic Boundary

This should not pull Frost Rocks into generic AI education yet.

For now, keep the anchor:

  • Geology first.
  • Place-based learning.
  • Real-world encounters.
  • User-authored observations and photos.
  • Content that improves noticing, memory, and discrimination in the field.

The broader pattern may be reusable later across other domains, but Frost Rocks should prove it through rocks, places, and field experience first.

Research / Design Questions

  • What does the learner model need to know before generated content becomes genuinely useful?
  • Which formats are worth testing first: essay, field brief, podcast/audio, quiz, guided comparison, or personalized mini field guide?
  • How do we avoid content that feels like generic AI slop with geology nouns sprinkled on top?
  • Can generated content cite or point back to the user's own captures, trusted sources, and known concepts?
  • Should the app generate content before an experience, after an experience, or during review?
  • How do we evaluate whether the content actually improves field noticing or identification skill?

Early Lean

Do not build this now.

Preserve it as a long-term Frost Rocks learning-engine direction. The near-term app still needs the core capture/review loop to prove itself in Finland. But when Izzy/Arya/Petra return to the post-Finland vision, this idea should be considered alongside:

  • AI Tutor.
  • Confusion-pair learning.
  • Knowledge capture progression.
  • External learning-source ingestion.
  • Home place vs. visited places.

The wedge version to test later: generate one short, place-aware learning brief for Andy that uses what Frost Rocks already knows about his captures, prior knowledge, and current confusions.
