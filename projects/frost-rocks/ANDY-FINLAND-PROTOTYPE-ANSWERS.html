Frost Rocks — Andy Answers On Finland Prototype

Captured by Izzy | June 2026

1. What would make opening the app feel worth it during the trip?

Andy said the strongest triggers are:

  • A place he wants to remember.
  • A question he cannot answer, or a question he has about the place.
  • Something that sparks curiosity and makes him want to know more.

This is not mainly about confusion. The moment might be beautiful, interesting, or curiosity-provoking. The core trigger is: "I see something, it sparks a question, and I want to know more."

2. Are you willing to make a guess or observation before seeing help?

Yes.

Andy likes the idea of being prompted with "What do you notice?" and wants a light back-and-forth:

  • "Hey, what is this?"
  • "Well, what do you think it is?"
  • "What do you notice?"
  • "What might it be?"

Design implication: attempt-first is acceptable, and even appealing, if it feels conversational and observational rather than like a formal quiz.

3. After Finland, what would make reviewing feel successful?

Successful review would include:

  • Remembering the place.
  • Consolidating knowledge.
  • Improving rock vocabulary.
  • Improving rock identification ability.
  • Building mental models.
  • Connecting new learning to previous knowledge.
  • Producing a journal.

Andy specifically referenced Make It Stick and the idea of building mental models.

Sidebar: Andy currently uses Diarium for journaling/diary. It would be valuable to explore whether Frost Rocks can export or integrate with Diarium in some way.

4. Should the app include lakes, forests, shorelines, buildings, churches, roads, and monuments when they help explain the geology?

Keep the prototype focused on geology for now.

Andy thinks the broader idea of a field journal for place-based learning generalizes beyond geology, but for Frost Rocks Finland, other points of interest and natural-history details should stay as background context.

Expansion vision to note: a generalized naturalist field journal for place-based learning.

5. Are you comfortable with answers like "likely gneiss, not confirmed," or do you want firmer IDs even when the app has to be less honest?

Andy prefers honesty and lower certainty, especially when uncertainty exposes important learning points.

Useful learning prompt:

> Why do you think this might be gneiss? Why can't you be certain? What would make you certain?

Design implication: uncertainty should not be treated as a failure state. It can be a powerful teaching moment.

6. Should review feel more like a quiz, an interview, or rebuilding the story of the trip?

A little bit of all three.

Andy is not yet sure which mode should dominate. This should remain open for prototype testing.

Initial design interpretation:

  • Quiz elements can support retrieval and vocabulary.
  • Interview elements can support reflection and metacognition.
  • Story-rebuilding can support place memory and emotional connection.

7. What trip constraints matter most: rain, gloves, walking with others, bad signal, low battery, ferry/car movement, limited time at stops?

Bad signal is the biggest named constraint.

The app should ideally work offline. Andy assumes some learning/context features may be AI or LLM-based and may not run locally, so the prototype needs an explicit fallback plan for weak or no connectivity.

Design issue to revisit:

> What happens when there is no connectivity for AI?

Minimum fallback:

  • Let Andy record the encounter.
  • Save photo, time, location, note, guess/question, confidence, and event tag locally.
  • Mark the encounter for later AI/context generation.
  • Let Andy come back to it when connectivity returns.

8. Would you do a tiny end-of-day reflection while traveling, or should review wait until after you're home?

Yes. Andy likes the idea of an end-of-day reflection.

Purpose:

  • Consolidate the day's learning.
  • Plant seeds for future reflection.
  • Preserve questions that emerged during the day.
  • Prepare encounters for later review after the trip.

9. Do you want an exportable trip artifact, like a journal, or is the learning loop itself enough?

Yes, Andy wants an exportable trip artifact.

This connects back to the earlier note about Diarium, which Andy already uses for journaling/diary. Frost Rocks should explore export or integration paths so trip encounters and reflections can flow into Andy's existing journaling practice.

10. What is the one thing this prototype must not do to your vacation?

It must not make Andy less connected to the place.

The purpose is to increase connection to Finland, not pull attention away from it.

In-field capture should not be laborious or overly engaging. It should be a quick in-and-out interaction: capture the observation, save the question, and return to the place. Deeper reflection and connection should happen later.
