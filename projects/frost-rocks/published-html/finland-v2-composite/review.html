The reframe worth naming: capture is now camera-first, not notebook-first. Frost Rocks behaves like a camera app that happens to be a field guide — the rock is the trigger, the shutter is the reflex, and thinking happens after the photo is safe.

Try these flows

  • The two-tap reflex: tap the center Capture button → you land straight in the live camera → tap the shutter. Two taps, photo taken. Time yourself.
  • The escape hatch: in the camera, notice the Library thumbnail (bottom-left) and Back — both present, neither in the fast path.
  • Enrich or bail: after the shutter you get a "keep moving / alone & curious" screen. Try SKIP once, and try ＋ add detail once.
  • Three review doors: open review via (1) a map pin, (2) the “Revisit this one” button inside an entry, and (3) the Review tab in the nav. Same destination, three intents.
  • See it fire: tap the ▲ button (top-right) to open the live instrumentation panel and watch events + metrics as you go.

What's intentionally fake

  • The camera is simulated — a stylized live-view, not real getUserMedia. This is by design for a first pass: you feel the two-tap flow anywhere, no permission friction. Once the flow's locked we go real (that's the native/Expo lane).
  • Rock "photos" are generated SVG swatches, not real geology.
  • Knowledge %s, the map, IDs, and dates are fixed sample data.
  • Instrumentation is prototype-only — events live in your browser (localStorage) and export to a JSON file. No server, no analytics backend, no data leaves the device.
  • Learning content (the "look again" scaffold, the gneiss-vs-granite tell) is illustrative, not a vetted curriculum.
