FR-V0 Review Guide

Prototype question

Does this feel like a travel field notebook with memory, or does it feel like homework?

What to react to

  • FR-V0-01 Trip Notebook: Timeline/map/event first, themes second. Does that match how you would use it in Finland?
  • FR-V0-02 Quick Capture: Does Capture read as a standard photo-picker flow rather than a destination screen?
  • FR-V0-03 Offline Fallback: Does save-first with optional guide-later feel trustworthy?
  • FR-V0-04 Encounter Detail: Does uncertainty feel like learning, or like the app dodging an answer?
  • FR-V0-06 Review Entry: Does the cold Review fork work: choose your own start vs. let the guide choose?
  • FR-V0-06 Review From Notebook: Does starting review from a notebook entry feel different enough from cold review?
  • FR-V0-06 Knowledge Map: Does the domain map help explain what the user is learning in geology?
  • FR-V0-07 End-of-Day Reflection: Is this useful consolidation, or too much during travel?
  • FR-V0-08 Journal Export: Does export feel correctly tucked away as an output/settings path?

Intentional fakes

No real camera, GPS, AI, storage, map, or export. Photos are stylized placeholders. The interaction is designed to test flow, hierarchy, and learning feel.

Design decisions in v0

  • Geology stays the spine. Broader place/nature context appears only when it explains stone, landform, material, or deep time.
  • AI is tested as a calm expert guide after the user's observation, not as instant identification.
  • Review now has two entry modes: cold nav entry and notebook-entry review.
  • Cold review has two branches: user-selected starting point and guide-selected starting point.
  • Review now adds connections to earlier learning, because that is where mental models start to form.
  • The geology domain map is only a first placeholder. FSRS and spaced repetition should attach later to mapped confusions and concepts.
  • Export is an output of the learning loop, not a first-class navigation item.

Evidence standard

Petra's bar is behavior: capture, reflection, review, and changed understanding. A beautiful archive is not enough.
