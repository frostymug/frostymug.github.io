Recommended Sequence

  1. Continue active AI tutor design. Keep the D3 evening debrief wedge and wizard-of-oz pilots moving. This is the strategic product bet.
  2. Make exports reviewable. Build an export package with structured encounter data, original/local photos, deterministic filenames, and a manifest.
  3. Preserve metadata. Add native-quality photo metadata preservation and explicit structured encounter metadata for fields like time zone.
  4. Add small capture recovery. Add note editing for existing encounters once the larger capture reliability problem is closed.
  5. Shape the knowledge inputs. Fold first-class questions into tutor design, then revisit dynamic tags/custom keywords as knowledge-model cleanup.

Priority Rationale

The order is intentionally not "shiniest first." AI tutor is the active strategic track, yes. The first field-fix layer is now done: capture reliability, photo inspection, and map inspection have landed. The remaining risk is the evidence substrate around export and metadata. Otherwise we will be asking a very clever tutor to reason over a partial field record. That is how expensive hobbies happen. We are trying to avoid those. Stoically.
