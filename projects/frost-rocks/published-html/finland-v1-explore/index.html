Four different takes on the same three anchor screens — Home (map + bottom nav), Capture (fast & alone modes), and Review/Knowledge. They differ in visual identity and in structure. Open each on your phone, react, and tell me which feels like Frost Rocks. Feedback anchors like FR-V1-A-REV are in each.

RUGGED · KRAFT
Direction A

Rugged Field Guide

Map-led. Warm paper, letterpress, stamped. The Finland map is the home; encounters are numbered guidebook entries.
NORDIC · MINIMAL
Direction B

Nordic Minimal

Timeline-led. Bright, airy, one teal accent. Calm vertical timeline, mini-map up top, progress as quiet bars.
ANALOG · DARK
Direction C

Analog Journal

Day-rhythm-led. Moody, photo-forward, serif. Organized around morning/night review; the trip reads as a story.
DATA · GRAPH
Direction D

Knowledge Graph

Graph-led. Clean, systemic, techy. Your expanding knowledge is the home surface; captures become linked nodes.
All four fix the same Build 1 gaps from Petra's interview: map is back, bottom nav with a central capture affordance, the two dead counters are gone (only “to revisit” survives), capture has a fast vs. alone/curious split via progressive disclosure, and each has a knowledge/expanding-understanding representation.
