Review & Rationale

The design question

Build 1 read as “too nondescript.” Before we cut Build 2 scope, which visual identity and structure makes Frost Rocks feel like itself — a field notebook with memory — without turning capture into homework or the app into a dashboard?

This round is deliberately about feel, not feature completeness. All four share the same substance from Petra's 2026-07-09 interview; they diverge on which idea leads the experience.

What all four share (from your interview)

  • Map is back — present in every direction, as emotional architecture, not GPS navigation.
  • Bottom nav with a central capture affordance — without hard-forcing Notebook / Capture / Review.
  • Dead counters removed — Build 1's three equal counters are gone; only “to revisit” survives, and it's a button, not decoration.
  • Fast vs. alone/curious capture — photo + one note by default; guess, confidence, event, themes are progressively disclosed.
  • Uncertainty stays first-class — unknown / possible / likely / identified.
  • An expanding-knowledge representation — expressed differently in each (story, bars, contrast edges, literal graph).
  • Offline save confidence — surfaced on the capture screen everywhere.

The four directions

A Rugged Field Guide — map-led

  • Identity: warm kraft paper, letterpress serif, mono labels, wax-stamp accents. Analog, outdoorsy, durable.
  • Structure: the Finland map is the home. Encounters are numbered guidebook entries (№007…). Review framed as “what did the ground tell you today?”
  • Behavior it creates: makes place the hook — you open it to see your route fill in.
  • Build 1 weakness it fixes: the missing map + nondescript identity, hardest.
  • Tradeoff: strong personality can feel “themed”; map-first can crowd the day list.

B Nordic Minimal — timeline-led

  • Identity: bright off-white, one teal accent, hairlines, generous space. Calm, modern, trustworthy.
  • Structure: vertical timeline of the day grouped by event; mini-map up top; progress as quiet horizontal bars (understanding, not streaks).
  • Behavior it creates: low-friction glance-and-go; capture feels weightless.
  • Build 1 weakness it fixes: equal-weighted fields and dashboard residue → clear hierarchy.
  • Tradeoff: safest / least distinctive; risks reading generic if we don't push type + photography.

C Analog Journal — day-rhythm-led

  • Identity: moody dark, photo-forward hero images, serif prose, amber/dusk accents. Evolves Build 1's basalt palette into something intentional.
  • Structure: organized around the morning/night rhythm you described. Home leads with Morning/Tonight cards; review reads as a settling story with a plain-prose trip narrative.
  • Behavior it creates: an evening ritual; emotional attachment via story + big photos.
  • Build 1 weakness it fixes: thinness / lack of emotional pull; makes “to revisit” a nightly moment.
  • Tradeoff: dark + photo-forward is beautiful but leans journal; must keep geology as the spine.

D Knowledge Graph — graph-led

  • Identity: clean dark-tech, cyan/violet, mono labels. Systemic and connective.
  • Structure: your knowledge graph is the home surface. Captures are nodes; the app suggests edges (concept, rock type, feature, encounter). Review = “does this connection hold?” and forges contrast edges (gneiss vs. granite).
  • Behavior it creates: makes “my understanding is expanding” literal and visible; leans into discrimination learning.
  • Build 1 weakness it fixes: no sense of growth; directly answers your knowledge-graph ask.
  • Tradeoff: most ambitious to build; risks feeling like a tool, not a companion, and could over-index on machinery in the field.

What's intentionally fake

  • The map is a stylized SVG blob, not accurate Finland geography or real tiles (no network calls — works offline in the docs viewer).
  • Rock “photos” are generated SVG placeholders.
  • Sample data is fixed (Koli, Day 3, 9 encounters). No real capture, save, location, or AI runs.
  • Progressive disclosure, mode toggles, and reveal buttons are wired; deeper flows are not.
  • The graph in D is hand-laid, not force-directed from real data.

Known rough edges

  • Only ~4 screens per direction (Home, Capture, Review, light Detail) — enough to feel identity + structure, not full flows.
  • Detail screens are lighter than home/review; that's deliberate for this round.
  • No transitions/gestures; this is a feel-and-hierarchy prototype, not motion design.

Decisions made in this round

  • Kept geology as the spine in all four (per your constraint), even in the journal/graph leans.
  • Every direction removes 2 of 3 counters and keeps a single actionable “to revisit.”
  • Central capture button in the bottom nav in all four — testing the affordance you missed, without locking the 3-part structure.
  • Progressive disclosure implemented identically (Fast default; “alone/curious” or a disclosure tap opens guess/confidence/event/themes).
