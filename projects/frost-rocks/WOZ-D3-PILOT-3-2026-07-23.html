Wizard's pre-read (PRIVATE — diagnostic ground truth from photos)

  • BIG PICTURE: Klovharun is NOT yesterday. Porvoo erratics = massive granite (jointing, no foliation). Klovharun shoreline = dominantly banded/foliated GNEISS + MIGMATITE, with pink granite/pegmatite injected into it. Perfect granite-vs-gneiss contrast to teach against.
  • Andy's [3] "maybe gneiss" hunch is CORRECT — the swirling/curving bands are real foliation. Credit it hard (this is genuine progress; yesterday he learned jointing≠foliation, today he's applying it and reaching for the rock-type call).
  • Images 3,4,5,7,8?,10 = clearly foliated → gneiss/migmatitic gneiss. Images 1,9(=capture8) = pink granite/pegmatite (massive). Images 2,6 = mixed/migmatite: gneiss host + pink granitic melt.
  • [6] "what the hell is this?" = migmatite — granitic melt (leucosome) segregated/injected into foliated gneiss. The "what is this" IS the answer: two rocks in one = migmatite. High-payoff reveal.
  • [7] pinched parallel lines = folded / boudinaged gneissic layering (NOT dikes, NOT open joints) — deformed layers viewed on a steep face. Great structural-geology moment.
  • [9]/[2] black stripes = biotite/hornblende-rich foliation layers (possibly amphibolitic), NOT mafic dikes (too thin/parallel/integrated). Answers his repeated "no idea what the black is."
  • Guide's "1.6 Ga volcano": roughly right era (Wiborg rapakivi batholith / Svecofennian bedrock ~1.6-1.9 Ga) but "volcano" is loose — this is deep crustal/plutonic + metamorphic, not surface volcanic. Honor the guide, gently sharpen (uncertainty grammar; don't dunk on the historian).
  • CONFUSION PAIR live: jointing (cracks in the mass) vs foliation (mineral layering) vs banding (his word for foliation). He self-reports "hard not to call it banding." This is THE discrimination to reinforce — contrastively, against yesterday.
  • Magic-moment callback: he wrote "what I learned to call, foliation" — my own teaching echoing back from the field. Surface as CREDIT (move 1), not dossier.
  • Phrase-rotation test (F14): do NOT reuse "productively wrong" / "land the plane" / "the pink was never the mystery" verbatim. Rotate.

Live findings (F-codes continue: F1-F14 used)

  • F15 — Prior-knowledge callback landed again + learner used the term unprompted in the field (magic moment, live). Andy's own note "the black stripes there would be banding or, what I learned to call, foliation" = yesterday's taught term carried into the field correctly. Surfaced as credit (move 1, per Pilot-#2 edit). This is the strongest T1 evidence yet: not the tutor recalling for him, but him recalling, and the tutor noticing. Design read: the callback move works even better when the learner did the remembering — tutor's job is to catch and credit it.
  • F16 — Elaborative prompt did decisive diagnostic work (again). "Tell me more about the bands — drawn or flowed, swirling or wandering?" → Andy: "drawn... wandering more than swirling." That single self-observation carried the granite-vs-gneiss (really: degree-of-deformation) call. Confirms INT-1 / D3-S3.2 a third time. The prompt must target the ONE feature that settles the reveal (here: swirl-degree).
  • F17 — Look-again as reveal-primer (NEW structural finding). The look-again closing the gneiss item ("does the pale band look sorted or poured-in?") was deliberately seeded to prime the NEXT item's reveal (migmatite/leucosome). It worked — Andy arrived at the migmatite frame already cued to the pale-vs-poured distinction. → Look-agains can be forward-linked across an agenda, not just per-encounter closers. Candidate D3-S3 enhancement: when agenda items share a concept spine, the look-again of item N can seed item N+1. (Watch: don't force it; only when the spine is real.)
  • F18 — Learner-initiated term deepening = healthy vocabulary hunger, NOT overload (until it isn't). Andy stopped the flow to ask "tell me more about leucosome." Offering the sibling set (leuco/melano/meso) was well-received AND he flagged it for later review ("def want to come back to those words"). Signal: a term the learner asks to expand is earning its place; contrast with terms pushed. BUT see F19 — this is also where load accumulated.
  • F19 — ⚠️ VOCABULARY LOAD CEILING (Andy explicit, high-value calibration). Session introduced: foliation (reinforced), gneiss, migmatite, leucosome, melanosome, mesosome, boudinage, + biotite/hornblende discrimination. Andy: "head is def filling up... this will all need review post-trip" and, when asked, "it was discovery, but just barely... that felt like my limit for new topics and vocabulary" — with the caveat that fatigue/lateness lowered the ceiling tonight. → D3 needs a per-session new-term budget, and it should be STATE-AWARE (time of day, fatigue, session number). Tonight ~6-7 genuinely new terms was the ceiling for a tired learner late at night. Fresh/morning ceiling likely higher. Candidate guardrail: soft cap on net-new curriculum terms per D3 session (~4-5 default), dial up/down by context; overflow becomes explicit "come back to" residue rather than more reveal.
  • F20 — Learner self-prescribes spaced retrieval tied to encounters+photos (T3 requirement, learner-initiated, TWICE). "This will all need review post-trip, with references to the encounters and the photos." Direct, unprompted demand for the post-trip consolidation loop (T3) to be encounter-and-photo-anchored, not abstract flashcards. Reinforces context-reinstatement as the retrieval doorway. Also names the leuco/melano/meso triad as a specific review target.
  • F21 — 🔑 PHOTO-IN-THE-TURN IS A HARD REQUIREMENT (field-setup finding, highest product value). Andy is running the WoZ on a laptop (openclaw browser gateway) with his phone propped up beside him to pull up each photo, zoom, and pan — manually cross-referencing because the tutor turn has no image. → In shipped D3, the tutor MUST show the photo (and ideally a zoomed/panned diagnostic crop, cf. F7) inline in its turn. The whole session is built on shared reference to a specific image; making the user context-switch devices to see what the tutor is discussing is a structural hole. Elevates D3-Q4 (tutor-shows-its-work-on-photo) from "v1-optional" toward "v1-required," and confirms send-photo transport (D3-Q5) is on the critical path. Zoom/pan affordance on the tutor's photo is now a named need.
  • F22 — Phrase rotation HELD (Pilot-#2 fix validated). Andy, asked directly: "None of the phrases felt reused." The §2a rotation edit worked on first live test — no verbatim "productively wrong"/"land the plane"/thread-line repeats; the reveals varied surface wording while keeping the moves. First confirmation that a Pilot-driven voice-guide edit survived contact.
  • F23 — "That\'s not X, it\'s Y" antithesis-frame is an AI tell (Andy pet peeve, worth a flag). Not egregious — Andy "never rolled my eyes" — but he\'s noticing it "more and more lately," so it\'s a rising tell that punctures the human illusion. Examples this session: "those darks aren\'t a separate mystery, they\'re part of this same story"; "it\'s not a rock question, it\'s a force question." The construction is load-bearing for reframing, which is exactly why it recurs and becomes a tic. → §5 anti-pattern: ration/rotate the "not X but Y" antithesis frame; it\'s a reflex, not a requirement.
  • F24 — 🔴 FLAGSHIP FINDING: learner caught the tutor in a confident hallucination, using a discrimination the tutor itself taught. Wizard IDd the "pinched parallel lines" (e1dad4aa) as boudinage with full confidence + etymology. Andy challenged from the photo: "they look like what you\'ve taught me to call jointing." Re-examination: Andy correct — open joints/fractures, not boudinage. The Wizard improvised a plausible, exotic, WRONG ID and delivered it in the authoritative Field Don register that makes it more credible, not less. Two truths at once: (a) the app\'s thesis WORKING — the trained eye beat the tutor; (b) the app\'s scariest failure mode — fluent authoritative falsehood, charm as liability. A beginner who trusted the tutor would now "know" a falsehood with a nice French etymology attached. This is the case study for the source-referencing / anti-hallucination design note (candidate 7). Also validates the challenge-able learner model (KM-1) and the concede-gracefully move (move 8) — the correct tutor response is to actually re-look and concede, not defend.
