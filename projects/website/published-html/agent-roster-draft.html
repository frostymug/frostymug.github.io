The coworkers behind the one-person practice.

The avatars should feel like internal team portraits, not mascots. The descriptions should make the coordination legible: who owns what, where human judgment enters, and how work moves.

The builder the agents report to.

open notebook

What the work is teaching us.

The notebook layer is texture and trust, not the main offer. Fellow builders get the signal. Project clients get proof that the system is being watched, questioned, and improved.

field note

The system is not the product.

The client does not need to care about every agent. They need confidence that the right work is happening behind the wall and that a person is accountable for the outcome.

field note

Receipts beat claims.

Build logs, review notes, and release traces make the practice feel real without pretending it is a mature agency with a lobby and a sales team.

field note

Learning in public needs edges.

Show enough of the notebook to build trust. Do not make the customer feel like the experiment. The experiment is the operating model; the promise is working software.
