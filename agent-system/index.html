Two Axes of Evolution

Axis 1 — Breadth

Stand up the three functions. Figure out how Andy works with each one.

  • What does a good session with each role look like?
  • What artifacts does each role produce?
  • How do handoffs between functions work?
Axis 2 — Depth (adversarial)

Introduce productive tension within and between roles.

  • Architect vs. Engineer
  • Designer vs. PM
  • QA vs. Engineer
  • Strategist vs. PM

Human-in-the-Loop Trust Ramp

1
Andy + one agent at a time
Andy is the integration layer between all functions. Full oversight.
2
Agents hand off to each other
Andy reviews the handoffs, not every decision within them.
3
Adversarial pairs run autonomously
Andy reviews outcomes, not process. Exceptions escalate up.
4
Andy is CPO again
Setting direction and reviewing exceptions only. Full delegation below.

Checks & Balances Patterns

Adversarial Review
QA and Engineer have explicitly different incentives baked into their prompts. They should disagree sometimes.
Cross-functional Sign-off
Designer and PM must agree before Engineering starts. No unilateral scope.
Red Team Agent
A dedicated agent whose only job is finding problems with everyone else's output.
Confidence Routing
Agents flag low-confidence decisions for human review rather than proceeding anyway.

The Core Loop (operational)
